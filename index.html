Terms Description

Absolute Magnitude
The absolute magnitude is a measure of luminosity of the asteroid, defined as the apparent magnitude that it would have if it were viewed from the distance of one astronomical unit from the Sun and the observer - the bigger the numerical value of the absolute magnitude, the less luminous the object.
Estimated Diameter (km/min)
The minimum estimated diameter for the asteroid, in kilometers.
Estimated Diameter (km/max)
The maximum estimated diameter for the asteroid, in kilometers.
Relative Velocity (km/s)
The relative velocity of the asteroid is defined as its velocity with respect to the Earth.
Orbit Uncertainity
The orbital uncertainty is related to several parameters used in the orbit determination process including number of observations and the quality of the observations - it is a logarithmic scale from 0 to 9 and the the larger the number, the larger the uncertainty.
Minimum Orbit Intersection
The minimum orbit intersection distance (MOID) is the distance between the closest points of the osculating orbits of two bodies.
Jupiter Tisserand Invariant
The value calculated from the semi-major axis, the orbital eccentricity and the inclination of the asteroid relative to Jupiter.
Orbit Eccentricity
Eccentricity of the ellipsis of the orbit.
Orbit Semi-Major Axis
Semi-major exis of the ellipsis of the orbit.
Orbit Inclination
The inclination of the orbit, the angle between the Earth's orbital plane and the asteroid's orbital plane.
Ascending Node Longitude
The angle from the origin of longitude (in a heliocentric orbits, is the First Point of Aries) to the ascending node (in heliocentric orbits, where the asteroid moves north through the ecliptic).
Orbital Period
The amount of time the asteroid takes to complete one orbit.
Perihelion Distance
The closest distance from the Sun.
Perihelion Argument
The angle to the asteroid's ascending node to its perihelion, measured in the direction of motion.
Aphelion Distance
The greatest distance from the Sun.
Mean Anomaly
The fraction of the orbit's period that has elapsed since the orbiting body passed perihelion.
Mean Motion
The angular speed required for the asteroid to complete one orbit.
Hazardous
A true or false value that indicates if the near-Earth asteroid is considered a potentially hazardous object, i.e., with an orbit that can make close approaches to Earth and large enough to cause significant damage in the case of an impact.
